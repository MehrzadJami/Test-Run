Continuous Stirred-Tank Bioreactor: Monod Kinetics Model

This document describes a mathematical model for a continuous stirred-tank bioreactor
(chemostat) operating under steady-state and dynamic conditions. The model uses Monod
kinetics to describe microbial growth on a single limiting substrate.

System Description:
A chemostat maintains constant volume by balancing feed inflow with effluent outflow.
Fresh medium containing substrate at concentration Sin enters at volumetric flow rate F,
giving dilution rate D = F/V. The bioreactor is well-mixed and operates isothermally.

State Variables:
- X: biomass concentration (g/L) — concentration of microorganisms in the reactor
- S: substrate concentration (g/L) — concentration of limiting nutrient (e.g. glucose)

Parameters:
- mumax: maximum specific growth rate = 0.40 1/h
- Ks: Monod half-saturation constant = 0.50 g/L
- Yxs: biomass yield coefficient = 0.50 g-X/g-S (grams of biomass per gram of substrate)
- D: dilution rate = 0.20 1/h (operator-controlled)
- Sin: inlet substrate concentration = 10.0 g/L

Governing Equations:
Specific growth rate (Monod):
  mu = mumax * S / (Ks + S)

Biomass balance:
  dX/dt = (mu - D) * X

Substrate balance:
  dS/dt = D * (Sin - S) - (1 / Yxs) * mu * X

Steady-State Solution:
At steady state (dX/dt = 0, dS/dt = 0):
  S_ss = D * Ks / (mumax - D)
  X_ss = Yxs * (Sin - S_ss)
Washout occurs when D >= mumax.

Assumptions:
- Perfect mixing in the reactor (no spatial gradients)
- Single limiting substrate; all other nutrients are in excess
- Constant reactor volume and steady feed composition
- Isothermal operation; growth rate depends only on substrate concentration
- No product inhibition; no maintenance energy requirements
- Cell death rate is negligible compared to growth rate

Limitations:
- Monod kinetics do not capture substrate inhibition at high concentrations
- Yield coefficient Yxs is assumed constant; in reality it may vary with growth rate
- Model does not include structured intracellular dynamics
- Not valid near washout (D → mumax) without additional considerations
- Single-substrate model only; does not account for co-limitation

Typical parameter ranges (aerobic heterotrophic bacteria):
  mumax: 0.2 – 1.5 1/h
  Ks: 0.05 – 2.0 g/L
  Yxs: 0.3 – 0.6 g-X/g-S
