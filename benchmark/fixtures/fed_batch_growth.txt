Fed-Batch Bioreactor Model with Variable Volume

This document describes a dynamic model for a fed-batch bioreactor, where substrate-rich
medium is added continuously while no liquid is withdrawn. The reactor volume therefore
increases over time, and dilution effects must be explicitly accounted for.

System Description:
A fed-batch bioreactor starts with an initial culture volume V0. Substrate feed is pumped
in at volumetric flow rate F with substrate concentration Sin. Biomass X and substrate S
concentrations are expressed per unit liquid volume. The culture grows according to
Monod kinetics while volume increases.

State Variables:
- X: biomass concentration (g/L) — microorganism concentration in liquid volume
- S: substrate concentration (g/L) — limiting substrate concentration in liquid volume
- V: liquid volume (L) — total liquid volume in the reactor at time t

Parameters:
- mumax: maximum specific growth rate = 0.35 1/h
- Ks: Monod half-saturation constant = 0.30 g/L
- Yxs: biomass yield on substrate = 0.45 g-X/g-S
- F: volumetric feed rate = 0.50 L/h (constant feed strategy shown here)
- Sin: feed substrate concentration = 50.0 g/L (concentrated feed)
- V0: initial liquid volume = 5.0 L

Governing Equations:
Specific growth rate (Monod):
  mu = mumax * S / (Ks + S)

Dilution rate (time-varying):
  D(t) = F / V(t)

Biomass balance (concentration):
  dX/dt = (mu - D) * X

Substrate balance (concentration):
  dS/dt = D * (Sin - S) - (1 / Yxs) * mu * X

Volume balance:
  dV/dt = F

The system starts from initial conditions X(0) = X0, S(0) = S0, V(0) = V0.
As V increases, D decreases, allowing accumulation of biomass while substrate is replenished.

Assumptions:
- Feed medium is sterile (no organisms in feed stream)
- Perfect mixing at all times despite increasing volume
- Monod kinetics describe growth; other growth models not considered
- Yield Yxs is constant across all growth rates
- No evaporation; liquid volume increases only due to feed addition
- No overflow; reactor vessel is large enough to hold final volume

Limitations:
- Constant feed rate F is a simplification; exponential or optimised feeding strategies
  give better performance
- Does not model oxygen or other nutrient co-limitations
- Structured cell models (cell cycle, intracellular storage) are not included
- Heat generation and cooling requirements are not modelled
- Volume increase is bounded by reactor geometry; model assumes unlimited capacity
- Viscosity changes due to biomass accumulation are neglected

Operational target: maximise total biomass X * V at harvest time.
