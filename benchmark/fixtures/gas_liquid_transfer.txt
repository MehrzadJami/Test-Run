Oxygen Transfer Model for Aerobic Bioreactors

This document describes a dynamic model for dissolved oxygen (DO) concentration in
an aerobic stirred-tank bioreactor. Oxygen supply from the gas phase and consumption
by the microbial culture are both accounted for.

System Description:
Oxygen is sparged into the bioreactor and dissolves at the gas-liquid interface. The
volumetric oxygen transfer coefficient kLa characterises the rate of oxygen dissolution.
Microorganisms consume dissolved oxygen at a rate proportional to biomass concentration X.

State Variables:
- C: dissolved oxygen concentration (g/L) — DO level in the liquid phase
- X: biomass concentration (g/L) — microbial cell concentration

Parameters:
- kLa: volumetric oxygen transfer coefficient = 100 1/h (depends on agitation, aeration)
- Cstar: dissolved oxygen saturation concentration = 0.008 g/L (at 30 °C, 1 atm air)
- qO2: specific oxygen uptake rate = 0.05 g-O2/g-X/h (oxygen demand per unit biomass)
- D: dilution rate = 0.10 1/h

Governing Equations:
Oxygen transfer driving force (Henry's law):
  OTR = kLa * (Cstar - C)

Oxygen uptake rate:
  OUR = qO2 * X

Dissolved oxygen balance:
  dC/dt = kLa * (Cstar - C) - qO2 * X - D * C

At steady state:
  C_ss = (kLa * Cstar - qO2 * X) / (kLa + D)

Critical dilution rate for oxygen limitation occurs when C approaches zero.

Assumptions:
- Perfect gas-liquid mixing; kLa is spatially uniform
- Henry's law applies; Cstar depends on temperature and partial pressure of O2
- Oxygen uptake rate is proportional to biomass (no substrate limitation modelled here)
- Isothermal operation
- Gas phase oxygen fraction is approximately constant (dilute transfer assumption)
- No oxygen storage in cells

Limitations:
- kLa is assumed constant; in practice it depends on agitation speed, airflow, and viscosity
- Does not model gas holdup or bubble size distribution
- qO2 is assumed constant; it varies with growth rate and substrate availability
- Model is valid only when C > 0 (aerobic regime); anaerobic transition not captured
- Temperature effects on Cstar are not included explicitly
