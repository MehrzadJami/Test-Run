Photobioreactor Model with Light-Limited Growth and Photoinhibition

This document describes a kinetic model for microalgal growth in a photobioreactor,
where light is the primary energy source. The model accounts for both light limitation
at low intensities and photoinhibition at high intensities using the Haldane-type
Andrews kinetic expression for light.

System Description:
A continuous photobioreactor (flat-panel or tubular) is illuminated at incident light
intensity I. Microalgae (biomass X) grow using light energy and CO2. Light is attenuated
within the culture according to Beer-Lambert law, but this model uses the surface
incident intensity as representative. The bioreactor is operated continuously with
dilution rate D.

State Variables:
- X: microalgal biomass concentration (g/L) — dry weight of algae per litre

Parameters:
- mumax: maximum specific growth rate at optimal light intensity = 0.25 1/h
- KI: half-saturation constant for light = 80 µmol/m²/s (light level for half-maximum growth)
- KiI: photoinhibition constant = 500 µmol/m²/s (inhibition coefficient for light)
- I: incident light intensity = 200 µmol/m²/s (photon flux density at reactor surface)
- D: dilution rate = 0.10 1/h

Governing Equations:
Light-limited specific growth rate (Haldane/Andrews kinetics for light):
  mu_L = mumax * I / (KI + I + I^2 / KiI)

Biomass balance (continuous operation):
  dX/dt = (mu_L - D) * X

At steady state:
  X_ss = (mu_L - D) * X = 0, so mu_L = D at steady state if X > 0

Optimal light intensity for maximum growth rate:
  I_opt = sqrt(KI * KiI)

At I = 200 µmol/m²/s, KI = 80, KiI = 500:
  I_opt = sqrt(80 * 500) = 200 µmol/m²/s (this fixture is at optimal conditions)

Assumptions:
- Incident light intensity I is uniform throughout the reactor (well-mixed optically)
- CO2 and nutrients are non-limiting (light is the sole limiting factor)
- Temperature is maintained at optimum; no thermal inhibition
- No dark respiration term (maintenance energy neglected)
- Cell settling and adhesion to reactor walls are negligible
- Biomass optical properties (extinction coefficient) are constant

Limitations:
- Light attenuation with depth (Beer-Lambert) is not explicitly modelled; the use of
  incident intensity I is only accurate for thin or dilute cultures
- Model does not capture diurnal light cycles (relevant for outdoor cultivation)
- Only single-species growth is modelled; competition dynamics are excluded
- Does not include nitrogen, phosphorus, or CO2 limitation
- Temperature and pH effects on growth rate are not modelled
- Haldane kinetics is an approximation; actual photoinhibition curves vary by species
